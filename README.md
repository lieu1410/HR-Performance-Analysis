# HR-Performance-Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes HR data from **2014–2019** to evaluate employee performance, compensation, productivity, and resignation trends.

## 🧹 Data Cleaning
- Handled missing values, duplicates, and inconsistent formats  
- Combined **Python processing + manual validation**  
- Categorized issues into **11 main cases**  
- Cleaned dataset documented via Google Sheets  

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Workforce Overview
- 292 employees across 31 roles  
- Average age: 48  
- Resignation rate: **24.32%**  
- Aging workforce → lack of young talent pipeline  

### 2. Pay & Performance
- Average salary: **~$72K/year**  
- Average bonus: ~$7.9K (~10%)  
- Positive correlation between income and performance  
- Managers earn significantly more than staff  

> 📌 Performance improves over time but at a slow pace  

### 3. Productivity Analysis
- Average performance score: ~3.0  
- Average satisfaction: ~3.85  
- Majority of employees fall into “Fully Meets”  

> 📌 Satisfaction strongly correlates with performance → improving engagement can boost productivity  

### 4. Resignation Analysis
- High turnover rate (~18–24%)  
- Average tenure before leaving: **1.57 years**  

#### 🔑 Key Drivers
- 💰 Higher salary (primary reason)  
- 📋 Unclear duties  
- ⚠️ Toxic work environment  
- 📦 Work overload  

#### 🔥 Hotspots
- Production department dominates resignations  
- Female employees account for ~60% of exits  
- High-performing employees are also leaving  

## ⚠️ Critical Findings
- Salary is **not the only retention factor**  
- High-performing employees are being lost  
- Turnover spikes in 2019 → potential internal issues  
- Weak onboarding and unclear career paths  

## 💡 Recommendations

### 1. Compensation & Benefits
- Offer more competitive salaries (especially Production & top performers)  
- Improve transparency in compensation structure  

### 2. Employee Retention
- Strengthen onboarding (first 4–6 months)  
- Develop clear career paths  
- Conduct regular employee feedback surveys  

### 3. Work Environment
- Clarify job roles and responsibilities  
- Reduce workload pressure  
- Enhance coaching and feedback culture
